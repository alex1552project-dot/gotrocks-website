How to Build a Fire Pit Area

Create the ultimate backyard gathering spot. A gravel fire pit area is safe, beautiful, and perfect for Texas evenings. The gravel provides a non-flammable surface while defining a cozy outdoor room.

1

Choose Location Wisely

Place fire pit at least 10-20 feet from structures, trees, and overhanging branches. Check local fire codesâ€”Montgomery County may have setback requirements. Avoid low spots where water collects.

2

Mark & Excavate the Area

Mark a circle 10-14 feet in diameter (for fire pit plus seating). Excavate 4-6 inches deep. A larger area gives you room for Adirondack chairs and a walking path around the pit.

3

Install Landscape Fabric

Lay heavy-duty weed barrier across the entire excavated area. Overlap seams by 6 inches and extend up the sides. This prevents grass and weeds from growing through your gravel.

4

Create the Border

Install edging around the perimeterâ€”steel landscape edging, large Bull Rock, or flagstones. This contains the gravel and defines the space. Bury edging 2-3 inches for stability.

5

Fill with Pea Gravel

Spread 3-4 inches of Pea Gravel throughout the area. Pea gravel is idealâ€”it's comfortable underfoot, drains well, won't blow away in wind, and won't pop or explode from fire heat.

6

Position Fire Pit & Seating

Set your fire pit in the center. Use flat stones or pavers under chair legs to prevent sinking. Leave 3-4 feet between fire pit edge and seating. Add accent lighting along the border for evening ambiance.

ğŸ’¡ Pro Tips from Texas Got Rocks

  • Pea gravel is best: Don't use river rock or lava rock near fireâ€”they can hold moisture and potentially crack or pop when heated. Pea gravel is the safest choice.
  • Create zones: Use different gravel colors or sizes to create a "ring" effectâ€”Bull Rock border, then pea gravel seating area.
  • Consider drainage: In Texas clay, add 2" of Limestone Base under the pea gravel for better drainage during heavy rains.
  • Fire safety: Keep a hose or fire extinguisher nearby. Never leave fires unattended. Clear area of leaves and debris regularly.
  • Furniture tips: Use flat stepping stones or concrete pavers as "pads" under heavy chairs and tables to prevent them from sinking into the gravel.
