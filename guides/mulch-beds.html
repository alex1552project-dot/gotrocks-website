How to Mulch Garden Beds

Give your landscape beds an instant facelift while protecting your plants. Fresh mulch suppresses weeds, retains moisture, regulates soil temperature, and makes everything look polished and professional.

üìã Step-by-Step Overview

1

Clear the Beds

Remove weeds, old mulch (if decomposed), and debris. Pull weeds by the roots‚Äîdon't just snap them off. Edge your beds for crisp lines using a half-moon edger or flat shovel.

2

Apply Pre-Emergent (Optional)

For maximum weed prevention, apply a granular pre-emergent herbicide before mulching. This creates a chemical barrier that stops weed seeds from germinating.

3

Calculate & Order Mulch

Measure your bed area (length √ó width). You need about 1 cubic yard per 100 square feet at 3" depth. Order 10% extra for irregular beds. Black Mulch for drama, Brown Mulch for natural look.

4

Dump & Distribute

Have mulch dumped as close to beds as possible. Use a wheelbarrow to move mulch to the beds. Dump piles throughout the bed rather than carrying from one spot.

5

Spread to 2-3" Depth

Use a rake to spread mulch evenly to 2-3 inches deep. Too thin won't suppress weeds; too thick can suffocate plant roots and hold too much moisture.

6

Keep Mulch Away from Stems

Pull mulch 2-3 inches away from plant stems and tree trunks. "Volcano mulching" against trunks causes rot and invites pests. Create a donut, not a volcano.

üí° Pro Tips from Texas Got Rocks

  • Black vs. Brown: Black mulch creates dramatic contrast and makes green plants pop. Brown looks more natural and blends with wooded settings.
  • Skip the fabric: Under mulch, landscape fabric often does more harm than good‚Äîit prevents mulch from enriching soil and creates a mess as it degrades.
  • Best time to mulch: Late spring after soil warms, or fall. Avoid mulching frozen ground or right before extreme heat.
  • Refresh annually: Add 1-2" of fresh mulch each year. Dyed mulches fade faster and may need refreshing twice yearly for color.
  • Water after: Give beds a good soaking after mulching to help mulch settle and start moisture retention.
