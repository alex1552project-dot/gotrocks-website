How to Build a Gravel Patio

Create a beautiful, low-maintenance outdoor living space. A gravel patio is one of the easiest and most affordable DIY projectsâ€”no concrete, no pavers, just simple layers that look amazing.

ğŸ“‹ Step-by-Step Overview

1

Plan & Mark Your Area

Decide on size and shape. Use stakes and string to outline the patio. Common sizes are 10x10, 12x12, or 10x14 feet. Consider furniture placement and traffic flow.

2

Excavate & Level

Dig down 4-6 inches within your marked area. Create a slight slope (1-2%) away from your house for drainage. Compact the soil with a tamper or plate compactor.

3

Install Landscape Fabric

Lay weed barrier fabric across the entire excavated area. Overlap seams by 6 inches. This prevents weeds from growing up through your gravel.

4

Add Base Layer

Spread 2-3 inches of Limestone Base or crushed stone. Compact thoroughly. This creates a stable foundation that won't shift over time.

5

Install Edging

Secure metal, plastic, or stone edging around the perimeter. This keeps your gravel contained and creates clean lines. Stake edging every 3-4 feet.

6

Add Decorative Gravel

Spread 2-3 inches of Black Star Gravel, Pea Gravel, or Decomposed Granite. Rake smooth and level. Add more in high-traffic areas.

ğŸ’¡ Pro Tips from Texas Got Rocks

  • Angular vs. round gravel: Angular gravel (like Black Star) locks together better and is more stable for furniture. Round gravel (like pea gravel) is softer underfoot but shifts more.
  • Depth matters: 2-3 inches of top gravel is ideal. Too thin and you'll see the base; too thick and walking becomes difficult.
  • Consider a fire pit: Plan for a fire pit area in the centerâ€”gravel patios are perfect for this since they're non-flammable.
  • Furniture pads: Use flat stones or pavers under furniture legs to prevent them from sinking into the gravel.
  • Maintenance: Rake occasionally to redistribute gravel and remove debris. Top off every 1-2 years as needed.
