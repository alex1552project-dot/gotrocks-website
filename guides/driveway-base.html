How to Build a Gravel Driveway

Create a durable, attractive driveway that handles Texas weather and heavy vehicles. Whether you're building new or refreshing an existing drive, the right layers and materials make all the difference.

ğŸ“‹ Step-by-Step Overview

1

Plan & Mark Layout

Stake out driveway boundaries. Standard width is 10-12 feet for single vehicle, 20-24 feet for two cars. Plan for slight crown in center (2-3" higher) for water runoff.

2

Excavate & Grade

Remove vegetation and excavate 8-12 inches down. Grade to ensure water flows away from structures. In Texas clay, consider installing a French drain alongside for drainage.

3

Compact Subgrade

Compact the native soil thoroughly with a plate compactor or roller. This prevents settling. If soil is soft, add 4-6" of Select Fill and compact.

4

Install Base Layer

Add 4-6 inches of Limestone Base or Granite Base. This is your structural foundation. Compact thoroughlyâ€”multiple passes. This layer does the heavy lifting.

5

Add Middle Layer (Optional)

For heavy traffic or soft soil, add 2-3" of 3/4" Limestone. This intermediate layer locks together and adds strength. Compact after spreading.

6

Apply Top Layer

Finish with 2-3 inches of 3/8" Limestone, Pea Gravel, or Decomposed Granite. Finer material fills gaps and creates a smooth driving surface. Maintain the crown.

ğŸ’¡ Pro Tips from Texas Got Rocks

  • Three-layer system: Base (large), middle (medium), top (fine). Each layer should be about half the size of the one below. This creates a stable, interlocking structure.
  • Compact each layer: Don't skip compactionâ€”it's the difference between a driveway that lasts 20 years and one that ruts in 2.
  • Texas clay consideration: Our clay soil expands and contracts. A thick base layer (6"+) is essential to prevent heaving and cracking.
  • Edge containment: Without borders, gravel migrates into your lawn. Steel edging, landscape timbers, or Belgian blocks keep gravel contained.
  • Maintenance: Rake and redistribute gravel annually. Add 1-2" of top layer every 2-3 years. Fill ruts and potholes immediately.
