Application Tips for Houston

Best Time to Apply

The best times to add topsoil in Houston:

  • Early Spring (February-April): Before planting season, gives soil time to settle
  • Fall (September-November): Ideal for lawn renovation before winter dormancy

Avoid adding topsoil during peak summer heat‚Äîit can dry out quickly and stress new plants.

Application Steps

  1. Remove any existing weeds, debris, or old sod
  2. Till or loosen existing soil to 2-3 inches if possible
  3. Spread topsoil evenly to desired depth
  4. Rake smooth and level
  5. Water lightly to settle, then plant or lay sod

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the black dye safe for my plants, pets, and kids? +
Yes. Quality black mulch (like ours) is dyed with carbon-based colorants‚Äîessentially the same compounds found naturally in soil. It's non-toxic and safe for vegetable gardens, pet areas, and playgrounds. We don't use recycled wood from treated lumber or pallets.
Will black mulch stain my concrete driveway or sidewalk? +
Fresh black mulch can temporarily stain light-colored concrete if wet mulch sits on it. We recommend keeping mulch 6+ inches from concrete edges and sweeping any stray pieces immediately. Any staining typically fades within a few weeks with normal weathering.
How long will the black color last in Houston's sun? +
Our black mulch maintains rich color for 6-9 months under normal Houston sun exposure. By month 10-12, it will fade to a dark gray. Most homeowners refresh their mulch annually, which is good practice regardless of color since mulch decomposes and thins over time.
Does black mulch attract termites? +
No more than any other organic mulch. Termites are attracted to wood in contact with soil, regardless of color. To minimize any risk, keep mulch 12+ inches from your home's foundation and maintain your regular pest control schedule.
How does black mulch compare to rubber mulch? +
Black hardwood mulch is natural, biodegradable, and feeds your soil as it decomposes. Rubber mulch doesn't decompose (good for playgrounds) but doesn't improve soil and can get extremely hot in Houston summers. For flower beds and gardens, we always recommend natural hardwood mulch.
