Expert Advice

Pro Tips for Limestone

Get the most out of your mulch with these professional landscaping tips

üí° Expect Initial Dust

Limestone is naturally powdery when first spread. The dust settles quickly and actually helps the material compact and bind together.

üí° pH Consideration

Limestone is alkalite and will raise soil pH over time. Keep it away from acid-loving plants like azaleas, blueberries, and rhododendrons.

üí° Compact Thoroughly

Limestone compacts to an almost concrete-like surface when properly installed. Water and compact in layers for best results.

üí° Great for Drainage

The powdery nature helps prevent weeds while still allowing water to drain. Still recommend landscape fabric for best results.
